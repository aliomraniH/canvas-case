1 · Highlights

  • The liraglutide (SCALE) band is now real trial statistics. The expected-response corridor for patients on liraglutide 3.0 mg previously used an illustrative width (published mean ×0.5/×1.5, disclosed as estimated). It is now the published 56-week distribution from Pi-Sunyer 2015 (NEJM 2015;373:11-22): mean −8.0%, SD 6.7 percentage points, drawn as −1.3% to −14.7%.
  • Honest labeling, upgraded. The legend changes from “Expected response (SCALE, estimated)” to “Expected response (SCALE, ±1 SD)”, and the ⓘ panel now states exactly what the band is and isn’t: published mean ±1 SD at 56 weeks, LOCF, trial completers — and that real-world response is right-skewed, so the symmetric band is an approximation.
  • Responder rates a clinician can quote. The disclosure carries the trial’s published categorical outcomes: ≥5% / >10% / >15% of body weight lost by 63% / 33% / 14% of patients respectively. The exact anchors (5%→63.2%, 10%→33.1%, 15%→14.4%) ship in SCALE metadata now, so rendering them as chart markers next version is a pure drawing change.
  • Nothing else moved. STEP-1 and SURMOUNT-1 bands are untouched and regression-guarded; this patch only replaces the one band that was not yet trial-derived.

4 · Observations & architectural findings

  • A boolean flag’s meaning can change while its value doesn’t. estimated_bounds stays True for SCALE, but it now flags the imputation/normality basis (a Gaussian ±1 SD approximation of a right-skewed distribution) rather than fabricated bounds. The semantic shift is recorded in the rationale so downstream consumers of the payload don’t read the old meaning into the new band.
  • Interpolation is a disclosed modeling choice. Pi-Sunyer 2015 publishes a 56-week SD but no per-week SDs, so intermediate weeks linearly interpolate between week 0 and the 56-week anchors — the band’s mid-course shape is a choice, not data, and the rationale says so.
  • Data-first, render-later worked. The three categorical CDF anchors landed as metadata only (Gate 5 deferred marker rendering): the clinically reviewed numbers are pinned in this version, and the v-next marker work cannot drift from them.
  • Basis matters as much as the numbers. The band describes 56-week LOCF outcomes among trial completers, not intention-to-treat — the disclosure copy states the basis explicitly so the band is not over-read.
